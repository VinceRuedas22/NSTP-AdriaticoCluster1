Plan 1: Sweep & Sip Days

“Sweep & Sip Days” is a biweekly community clean-up program that combines environmental action with social engagement. It offers simple incentives—like hot drinks, snacks, and hygiene kits—to encourage participation, particularly in low-income communities. Organized in partnership with barangay councils and NGOs, the program promotes bayanihan (communal unity) and aims to foster long-term behavioral change by turning routine clean-ups into positive, habit-forming experiences. While incentives boost turnout, concerns about dependency on material rewards highlight the need for additional strategies such as public recognition, education, and community involvement in planning. To maintain sustainability, the program is advised to start with monthly events, seek local donations, assign flexible roles, and rotate responsibilities to avoid burnout. These steps aim to strengthen community ownership and ensure the initiative’s lasting impact.

Plan 2: Inob-aksyon (Inobasyon + Aksyon)

“Inob-aksyon” is a monthly innovation challenge under Project 668 that encourages community members to develop creative, practical solutions to local waste issues—such as recycled crafts, DIY compost bins, or homemade drainage filters. Winning entries receive useful prizes like groceries, phone load, or eco-friendly products, depending on available support. Rooted in participatory development, this initiative empowers residents—especially in marginalized areas—to become active problem-solvers, fostering resilience and local ingenuity. Beyond showcasing innovation, Inob-aksyon also serves as a platform for education through workshops on sustainable practices, turning waste management into an opportunity for learning, empowerment, and community transformation.
